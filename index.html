5G Advance and 6G

6G Mission: India's Pursuit of Leadership in Next-Generation Wireless Technology
As the International Telecommunication Union (ITU) embarks on the development of the IMT2030 vision, which is the technical foundation for 6G technology, it is essential for India to play a pivotal role in shaping the future of global wireless standards. By taking the lead in defining 6G and its key performance requirements, India can influence global standards within ITU and 3GPP through enhanced research and development efforts.
The Indian Institute of Technology Hyderabad (IITH) is at the forefront of this mission, spearheading the development of prototypes for critical building blocks of 6G Radio Access Network (RAN) technology. These advanced components include extreme massive MIMO, distributed MIMO, innovative waveforms, and converged terrestrial and satellite systems.
By fostering an environment of innovation and collaboration, IITH aims to propel Indian technologies into the next generation of wireless communication standards. This will not only support the development of an indigenous technology base but also contribute to the deployment of advanced systems both in India and across the globe.
India's pursuit of leadership in 6G technology demonstrates the nation's commitment to staying at the cutting edge of wireless communication advancements. As we progress towards a connected future, the efforts of IITH and other research institutions will play a vital role in shaping the global landscape of next-generation wireless technology.

5G+ and 6G ORAN Base Station

A new type of radio access network based on an emerging technology called as ORAN (Open-Radio-Access-Network) is being a strong contender as the next major disruptor in the 5G landscape. This technology allows rapid deployment of low-cost, software upgradable 5G base stations in significantly higher volumes and larger densities than the current 4G network. ORAN is a software defined system based on open interfaces and general-purpose hardware. Some operators have initiated the deployment of ORAN based software-defined network (SDN) and virtualized networks that enable self-organization, low operational cost and ease of introduction of new features and service upgrades. New use cases can be introduced rapidly on the fly using software upgrades, as opposed to costly and time-consuming hardware development cycles.

Massive MIMO (mMIMO) technology is a current reality and it is included as an integral part of 5G physical layer (PHY). 5G offers a substantial capacity and coverage increase over 4G mainly by using a large number of antennas at the base station. This project develops a mMIMO base station stack that is compliant to 3GPP and ORAN specifications.

6G End-to-End Communication

The main aim of the project is to develop fundamental technology innovations and solutions in two important areas:

  • Maximization of Network coverage and range at high operational frequencies
  • Development of wireless technology that offers extremely high data rates and extremely low latency that offers near wireline (fiber) experience

The key objectives are:

  • Early prototyping and IPR generation in the above areas to generate 6G SEPs
  • Help Indian telcos and vendors offer a lead in the 6G product development through 6G POC development
  • Creation of highly trained man power

5G+/6G Converged Terrestrial and Satellite IoT (5G+/6G-sIoT)

This project aim is to develop radio communication technologies that can potentially form the backbone of 6G IoT technologies. The project aims to develop prototype products leading to commercialization of Converged Terrestrial and Satellite Communication and IoT technologies. This prototype supports Satellite communication as well as Internet-of-Things (IoT) modems that are implemented using a combination of 5G cellular, and 5G+ and 6G Satcom standards or proposed standards.

Next Generation Wireless Research and Standardization on 5G and Beyond

The aim of the project is to conduct research in areas which areas part of 5G and beyond, the research outputs will be filed as patents, publications. Standardization of all relevant research outcomes will be taken to the Indian standards forum TSDSI and the global standard forums like 3GPP, ITU and IEEE to incorporate it as SEPs. Also, building 5G and Beyond Solutions i.e. developing appropriate wireless systems and subsystems for Indian defense and telecom equipment vendors in India. Implementing the relevant algorithms developed through this project in the 5G testbed and building hundreds of experts in the area of 5G and beyond technologies in various Industries, Research and Development organizations.
