6G Mission: India's Pursuit of Leadership in Next-Generation Wireless Technology
As the International Telecommunication Union (ITU) embarks on the development of the IMT2030 vision, which is the technical foundation for 6G technology, it is essential for India to play a pivotal role in shaping the future of global wireless standards. By taking the lead in defining 6G and its key performance requirements, India can influence global standards within ITU and 3GPP through enhanced research and development efforts.
The Indian Institute of Technology Hyderabad (IITH) is at the forefront of this mission, spearheading the development of prototypes for critical building blocks of 6G Radio Access Network (RAN) technology. These advanced components include extreme massive MIMO, distributed MIMO, innovative waveforms, and converged terrestrial and satellite systems.
By fostering an environment of innovation and collaboration, IITH aims to propel Indian technologies into the next generation of wireless communication standards. This will not only support the development of an indigenous technology base but also contribute to the deployment of advanced systems both in India and across the globe.
India's pursuit of leadership in 6G technology demonstrates the nation's commitment to staying at the cutting edge of wireless communication advancements. As we progress towards a connected future, the efforts of IITH and other research institutions will play a vital role in shaping the global landscape of next-generation wireless technology.

5G+ and 6G ORAN Base Station: Revolutionizing Wireless Networks with Open-Radio-Access- Network Technology

The future of wireless communication is on the horizon, and Open-Radio-Access-Network (ORAN) technology is poised to be a major disruptor in the 5G landscape. This project focuses on developing a new type of radio access network based on ORAN, which promises to enable rapid deployment of low-cost, software upgradable 5G base stations in significantly higher volumes and larger densities than the current 4G network.

ORAN is a software-defined system built on open interfaces and general-purpose hardware. Several operators have already initiated the deployment of ORAN-based software-defined networks (SDN) and virtualized networks that enable self-organization, low operational cost, and ease of introducing new features and service upgrades. This approach allows for rapid introduction of new use cases using software upgrades, as opposed to costly and time-consuming hardware development cycles.

Massive MIMO (mMIMO) technology is a key component of the 5G physical layer (PHY), offering substantial capacity and coverage improvements over 4G by utilizing a large number of antennas at the base station. The mMIMO base station stack is a highly complex technology that includes intricate interactions between the Physical Layer and the protocol stack, a level of expertise currently possessed by only a select few multinational vendors. This project aims to develop an indigenous mMIMO stack that will enable India to effectively compete with the world's leading players in this space. By embracing ORAN technology and fostering innovation in 5G+ and 6G base stations, we are paving the way for a more connected, efficient, and adaptable wireless communication ecosystem.

6G End-to-End Communication: Pioneering Next-Generation Wireless Connectivity

The primary objective of this project is to develop ground breaking technology innovations and solutions in two crucial areas for the next generation of 6G networks:

  • Maximization of network coverage and range at high operational frequencies of 6 GHz and above.
  • Development of wireless technology that offers extremely high data rates and ultra-low latency, providing a near wireline (fiber) experience

To achieve these goals, the project focuses on developing an experimental prototype 6G communication system with the following key features:

  • A novel waveform that delivers high power amplifier efficiency, ultra-low latency, and extremely high data rates
  • A network architecture that employs a series of relays integrating wireless backhaul and radio access, aimed at developing a 6G Integrated Access and Backhaul (IAB) system that enables internet access in areas lacking fiber connectivity

5G+/6G Converged Terrestrial and Satellite IoT (5G+/6G-sIoT): Paving the Way for Ubiquitous Connectivity

Low Bit Rate Wireless Communications has emerged as a key driver for wide-area cellular IoT, encompassing applications such as smart metering, asset and animal tracking, perishable goods management, agri-tech, healthcare, and more. While terrestrial 5G networks provide reliable connectivity in densely populated areas, a significant portion of the world remains unconnected. In recent years, the remarkable reduction in satellite launch costs has paved the way for achieving global, ubiquitous connectivity.

In this project, the Indian Institute of Technology Hyderabad (IITH) is developing a Narrowband IoT (NB-IoT) System-on-Chip (SoC) that supports both terrestrial and Geostationary Orbit (GEO) satellite-based 5G massive Machine-Type Communication (mMTC) services. The SoC is designed to support up to 200 kHz RF bandwidth and 3GPP Release 13/14 specified frequency bands in both low and high cellular bands, including the L-band for enabling GEO Satcom connectivity.

This low-cost IoT SoC aims to address the majority of low-bit rate IoT applications expected to emerge in India and around the world in the coming years. By leveraging the convergence of terrestrial and satellite IoT technologies, IITH is pushing the boundaries of connectivity, helping to bridge the digital divide and enable innovative IoT solutions across various industries.

Advancing Next Generation Wireless Research and Standardization for 5G and Beyond

The primary objective of this project is to drive research in cutting-edge areas related to 5G and beyond, transforming research outcomes into patents, publications, and standards. By collaborating with the Indian standards forum TSDSI and global standard forums such as 3GPP, ITU, and IEEE, the project aims to incorporate relevant research findings as Standards Essential Patents (SEPs).
In addition to research and standardization, this project focuses on developing tailored wireless systems and subsystems for Indian defence and telecom equipment vendors. This involves implementing algorithms developed through the project in the 5G-advanced testbed to create innovative solutions for the Indian market.
Another key aspect of this project is building a robust talent pool of experts in 5G and beyond technologies, working across various industries, research, and development organizations. This will ensure that India remains at the forefront of next-generation wireless research and innovation, driving advancements in the global landscape of wireless communication.
